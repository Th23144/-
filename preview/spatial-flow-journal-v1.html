The editor's letter.

Past letters →
— Issue Vol I · 03 · Spring 2026 — Theme On thresholds — Pieces Six essays · 2 field notes — Reading time Roughly 38 minutes, unhurried

This issue began with a doorway. Not a large one — the one into our office, a plain wooden thing with a small stone balanced on the sill. Someone put it there in January. Nobody moved it. By March, it had quietly reorganized the way we stepped into our own days.

We realised we had been writing, for three years, about the same thing in different disguises: how objects you barely notice end up running the mood of a room — and how rooms, in turn, run the mood of the people in them. This is not new. Feng shui said it. Environmental psychology said it. Your grandmother said it.

We only hope to say it calmly, in English, without the glitter — and to keep publishing, slowly, until we have said it well.

— Spatial Flow
